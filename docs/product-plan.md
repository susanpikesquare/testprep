# Product Plan

## Audience

The first user is Nick: someone preparing for a GED or High School Equivalency exam who has already started to believe passing is impossible. The app should be useful academically, but its deeper job is to help him trust that he can learn again.

## Core Loop

1. Open the app.
2. Tap one clear "Start Today" action.
3. Complete a tiny session.
4. Get specific feedback.
5. See one piece of progress.
6. Leave before feeling drained.

## MVP Screens

### Today

The home screen. Shows one recommended tiny session, current streak, and one recent win.

### Practice

Short question sets by skill. Early sessions should be untimed. Timed mode comes later.

### Explanation

Shows whether the answer was correct, then explains the skill in direct, low-pressure language.

### Skill Map

Tracks GED areas by concrete skills instead of intimidating subject labels.

### Wins

Keeps a history of progress moments, such as "You got a fraction problem right that you missed before."

## GED Subject Areas

- Mathematical Reasoning
- Reasoning Through Language Arts
- Science
- Social Studies

## ADD-Friendly Requirements

- One primary action per screen.
- Short sessions by default.
- No dense paragraphs during practice.
- Visual progress indicators.
- Optional break prompts.
- Gentle return state after gaps.
- Minimal notification pressure.

## Confidence Language

Use:

- "Let's try one."
- "Good instinct. Let's fix the last step."
- "This is a practice question, not a verdict."
- "You came back. That counts."

Avoid:

- "Incorrect."
- "Failed."
- "You should know this."
- "Easy."
